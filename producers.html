Producers

Jared Keffer is a software developer and composer who produces music under the name “sloths”. After graduating from UOP Conservatory of Music, he created NightSky to weave art and software together into intriguing new experiences. His music aims to bring a whimsical state of flow to the listener, suspending them in feelings ranging from elation to serenity. His influences include Tycho, deadmau5, Seven Lions, Liquicity, Porter Robinson, Baths, and Steve Reich. His games are all about falling into your own groove, letting the user explore their experience through customization options and easy to learn but hard to master gameplay mechanics. Game development influences include Simogo, Supergiant Games, Mojang, and Santa Monica Studio.

An alumnus of University of the Pacific's Conservatory of Music, Thomas M.L. Ravago blurs lines across a wide variety of genres to create a unique sound within his music. Thomas draws upon a unique skillset as a multi-instrumentalist, composer, sound designer, and music producer. He is currently working as a music producer for 3013 Music & Media Group in Santa Monica, CA.

Andrew Clark, who goes by the producer name “Blinders,” is an EDM producer, composer, and jazz pianist from Roseville, CA. As an avid music listener, he draws inspiration from many different artists and styles to arrive at his varied sound. The name Blinders itself is a reference to this philosophy of drawing on many different genres for inspiration. When starting out as a producer, Andrew didn’t want his music to fit narrowly into one genre, so for this project, he decided to remove his musical “blinders” and pursue a broader vision that’s based around personal experimentation and inspiration from his favorite artists. While his music spans several different genres, his primary influences include Jacob Collier, Thundercat, San Holo, Duskus, Anomolie, and others. When producing music, his primary goal is to create music that is worth every second that listeners spend listening to it. I hope you enjoy! -Blinders
